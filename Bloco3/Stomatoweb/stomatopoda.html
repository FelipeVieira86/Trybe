Facts about Mantis Shrimp

Scientific classification

Odontodactylus scyllarus

  • Kingdom: Animalia
  • Phylum : Arthropoda
  • Subphylum : Crustacea
  • Class : Malacostraca
  • Subclass : Hoplocarida
  • Order : Stomatopoda

    Habitat

    The Mantis Shrimp lives in warm, shallow water, and typically grows to be between 6 - 12 inches in length.

    Nuclear bomb of light and beauty.

    While humans have three types of color-receptive cones, the mantis shrimp has SIXTEEN color-receptive cones.

    Came in like more than a wrecking ball.

    It has two raptorial appendages on the front of its body that accelerate with the same velocity as a gunshot from a twenty-two caliber rifle, and in less than three-thousandths of a second can strike prey with 1,500 Newtons of force.

    Throwing into orbit

    To put this in perspective, if human beings could accelerate our arms at 1/10th that speed, we'd be able to throw a baseball into orbit.

    The fastest hands in the ocean

  • Their limbs move so quickly the water around them boils in a process known as syoercavutation. When theses cavitation bubble collapse it produces an undersea shockwave that can kill prey even if the mantis shrimp misses its target.

    • The power in his hands

    The force of there collapsing bubbles also produces temperatures in te range of several thousand Kelvins and emitis tiny bursts of light. This effect is called sonoluminescence.
